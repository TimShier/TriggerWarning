Welcome to Trigger Warning! This handy extension for your browser is designed to help make your social media a little safer by altering words and phrases that you might find triggering. While it cannot prevent exposure to all triggering content, we are continuously striving to improve the service to help users like you.

What Trigger Warning will do:

  • Allows you to select potential triggers from a list of terms.
  • Replaces those terms with safer alternatives, highlighting those changed terms in a colour of your choice.
  • Allows you to add an additional trigger unique to you.

What Trigger Warning cannot do (yet):

  • Modify any sites other than Twitter (Facebook etc is coming)
  • Replace triggering images or full descriptions of triggering content.

What Trigger Warning will not do:

  • Block you from accessing potentially triggering content.
  • Be 100% accurate (we're still in Beta)

What now?

Click the button below then modify your setting so TriggerWarning only blocks the intended content.

 

About TriggerWarning

As users of the internet, we, ourselves, experience triggering content which, inadvertently, ends up in our timelines. This causes unnecessary anxiety which can stay with us for the rest of the day (or weeks or months). We're not fans of this!

TriggerWarning is an Open Source project curated by CitizenDialogueCentre. At it's core, TriggerWarning aims to help reduce specific triggering content from social media feeds (just Twitter so far).

Furthermore, as part of CitizenDialogueCentre's activity, the NGO will be facilitating some difficult (and triggering) conversations on various topics. Being able to manage your own trigger means you can participate in the general conversation without the risk of unexpectedly kicking off an anxiety.

Please note: TriggerWarning is still in Beta. Please read the disclaimer. It does a good but not perfect job and some of the pages are a bit clumbsy from a layout perspective - we're getting there :).

How to Contribute

TriggerWarning is an open source project for which EVERYBODY can contribute. There a couple of ways to help

  • Code: If you can wield The Javascript, have Chrome Extension experience or just want to learn then please get in touch. This is one of many projects and your skills would be appreciated!
  • Categories: Selecting default categories (and the associated triggering words etc) is actually really difficult (particularly that, in some cases, the language used around a topic varies and needs constant updating).
  • Ideas: This tool came out of a 3min conversation with somebody who was constantly being triggered. If you have ideas, for this or any other socially beneficial solution, please contact us!
  • Fixes: If you spot an issue (anything from a typo on the site to the extension not working), please let us know so we can resolve it.
  • Share: The easiest way to contribute is simply to share the extension with your friends/family or anybody else who might get benefit from it. The purpose here is to assist as many people as possible so please share it generally (with the link at the top of the page) or send the link to specific individuals who might benefit.
